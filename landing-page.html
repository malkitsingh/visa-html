How To Apply Visa

Prepare Required Documents

Fill Application Form

Final Step

Passport: Sample
  • Original Passport valid for at least 6 months issued within the last 10 years with at least two blank pages back to back. Old Passports, if any
Cover Letter: Sample
  • Original covering letter from the applicant stating the applicant’s name, professional status, passport details, purpose and duration of visit. For self-employed, the covering letter on business letterhead
Leave Certificate: Sample
  • Original Leave Sanction certificate from the company
Proof of Financial Documents: Sample
  • Income Tax Returns for last 3 years.
  • Original Updated Bank Statement for last 6 months with bank seal and signature.
  • Salary Slips for the last 3 months.
  • Supporting Financial Documents such as Fixed Deposits, Property Investments, Other Investments etc
Photo Specification: Sample
  • Two recent color photographs not older than 6 months with 35 x 45mm (white background). Photograph should be in matt finish and face should cover 80% of the photo area

Special Cases (Additional Supporting Documents)

If Self-Employed:
  • Registration certificate of business / Certificate of Incorporation / Partnership Deed.
If the applicant is sponsored:
  • Sponsorship letter.
  • Financial documents with passport copy of sponsor.
If the applicant is retired:
  • Pension statements for the last three months.
If visiting Family/Friends:
  • Invitation letter.
  • Passport and visa copy of the inviter or residence permit copy.
If the applicant is a Minor:
  • Minors, if traveling with None of the parents, Original notarized NOC required from both parents in the form of an affidavit on a stamp paper and a copy of their government photo ID.
  • Minors, if traveling with one parent, Original notarized NOC is required from the other parent in the form of an affidavit on a stamp paper and a copy of their government photo ID.
If Student:
  • Bonafide certificate or School/ College ID card copy.

Security Regulations

Free Import

  • For Non European Union Nationals (E.U), Alcoholic beverages having 22% alcohol– up to 1 liter or beer up to 16 liters or wine (not sparkling – 4 liters or fortified/ sparkling – 2 liters), per person aged 17 and over is allowed.
  • For Non European Union Nationals (E.U), Tobacco of all types in the following capacity– 200 cigarettes or 100 cigarillos or 50 cigars or loose Tobacco up to 250 grams per person aged 17 and over is allowed.
  • If you are travelling from any country within the European Union National (E.U), you may bring in any meat, dairy or other animal products. You may also bring in any fruit, vegetables or plant products other than plants and seeds of Fraxinus (Ash) intended... for planting as long as they are, grown in an EU Member State, free from pests and diseases and are for your own use or consumption.
View more

Restricted

  • Satellite phones carried in baggage/ hand baggage without the necessary Department of Telecommunications license shall be confiscated until such a license is provided.
  • All persons carrying valuable goods to U.K. must fill in a declaration with the detailed description of the imported goods and present it to the Customs service at the first point of entry.
  • Controlled Drugs such as cocaine, heroin, MDMA (Ecstasy), cannabis, barbiturates, amphetamine and methamphetamine certain other drugs with a high potential for abuse may not be carried by the traveler. If you need medicines that contain potentially addictive drugs or narcotics, declare all... drugs, medicinal, and similar products to the appropriate custom/immigration official. Carry such substances in their original containers and carry a prescription from your physician that the substances are being used under a doctor's supervision and that they are necessary for your physical well being while traveling.
View more

Prohibited

  • Children under 18 years of age traveling to the United Kingdom on an ACCOMPANIED visa must be accompanied by the person whose name or passport number is specified in the visa. If the child is accompanied by a different person, then the visa will not be valid.
  • Persons with a dual citizenship must use the same ID/travel/passport document for entering or leaving United Kingdom.
  • While transiting, if the airport of arrival differs from the airport of departure, a valid transit visa will be required before you begin your travel. You will have to check with the traveling airline for more details with respect to visa waiver program for transit, if any.
